Our Mission & Story

Driven by data, powered by community, dedicated to a cleaner Nairobi.

Why We Started

In 2024, a group of students and developers in Nairobi noticed a gap: we had plenty of complaints about air quality, but very little actionable data.

EcoWatch was born out of a simple idea: "You can't fix what you don't measure." We set out to build a platform that combines low-cost IoT sensors, satellite data, and community reporting to create a real-time picture of pollution in our city.

Today, we are a growing movement of coders, environmentalists, and citizens working together to hold polluters accountable and drive policy change through evidence.

Our Core Values

Data-Driven

We rely on hard evidence, not anecdotes. Our decisions and reports are backed by verified sensor data and scientific analysis.

Community First

Technology is useless without people. We empower local communities with the tools and knowledge to protect their health.

Transparency

All our data is open-source. We believe everyone has the right to know the quality of the air they breathe.

Innovation

We leverage AI, IoT, and modern web technologies to solve age-old environmental problems in new ways.

Our Journey

From a classroom project to a city-wide initiative.

2024

The Idea

EcoWatch begins as a final year project at the Coders' Symposium. Initial prototype built with 3 Arduino sensors.

Early 2025

Pilot Launch

Deployed first 10 sensors in Industrial Area and Dandora. Partnered with 2 local schools for data collection.

Late 2025

Platform Release

Launched the public dashboard and mobile reporting app. Reached 1,000+ active users in Nairobi.

2026 (Now)

City-Wide Impact

Network expanded to 50+ sensors. Data cited in Nairobi County Air Quality Action Plan. Expanding to Mombasa.
