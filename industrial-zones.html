Industrial Zones Analysis

Tracking emissions, PM2.5 levels, and environmental impact across Nairobi's key industrial hubs.

Why Focus on Industrial Zones?

While traffic is a major contributor, industrial activities account for ~15% of Nairobi's PM2.5. These zones often neighbor residential areas, creating significant health risks due to unchecked emissions of SO₂, NO₂, and heavy metals.

Zone Profiles

Detailed breakdown of activities and specific risks per zone.

1. Nairobi Industrial Area (Central)

High Risk

Location: South-central Nairobi, near Mombasa Road.

Activities: Metal works, chemical processing, food manufacturing, textiles.

Key Risks:

  • High PM2.5 from informal "jua kali" welding.
  • Chemical runoff into Nairobi River.
  • Diesel generator reliance during power outages.
Report Violation

2. Athi River / Export Processing Zone

Moderate-High

Location: ~25km SE of Nairobi.

Activities: Cement production, steel mills, large-scale manufacturing.

Key Risks:

  • Fugitive dust from cement kilns.
  • Heavy truck traffic congestion.
  • Regional haze contribution.
Report Violation

3. Dandora & Kariobangi

Critical

Location: East Nairobi.

Activities: Informal recycling, plastic melting, e-waste burning.

Key Risks:

  • Toxic fumes from burning plastics/e-waste.
  • Heavy metal contamination in soil/water.
  • Proximity to dense residential settlements.
Report Violation
